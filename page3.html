Red Christmas

On Christmas Day in London town,
A tale unfolded, dark and brown.
I, a stranger in a foreign place,
Fate dealt a card, an unexpected chase.

Face down, I met the cold, hard floor,
My front tooth shattered, dreams no more.
First day in London, a city grand,
But fate had other plans, not as planned.

To the hospital's room of urgency,
Rushed I was, in a state of emergency.
Bloodied tooth, a consciousness waning,
On Christmas Day, destiny was reigning.

Through the chaos, pain, and despair,
A dentist's touch, a tender care.
A brand-new tooth, a Christmas gift,
Amidst the turmoil, a spirits lift.

London, a city of memories and strife,
A chapter closed, a different life.
Yet, in the echoes of that fateful day,
I carry a tooth, a reminder to stay.

London may not suit, a truth so clear,
Yet, in its heart, a tale sincere.
On Christmas Day, a tooth reborn,
In London's grasp, a soul was torn.
